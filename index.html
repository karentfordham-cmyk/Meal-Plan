Meals
Garlic‑Lime Tempeh & Kale–Shiitake Stir‑Fry with Cauli‑Rice
330 kcal · 36g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 170 g Tempeh
  • 120 g Kale
  • 120 g Shiitake mushrooms — sliced
  • 2 cloves Garlic — minced
  • 1 tbsp Tamari / soy sauce (reduced sodium)
  • 0.5 each Lime — juice + zest
  • 10 g Ginger — grated
  • 0.5 tsp Chilli flakes — to taste
  • 1 tsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • 300 g Cauliflower (for rice) — grated/riced
  • 1 each Spring onion — optional garnish
Method / flavour notes
Pan‑sear tempeh in 1 tsp olive oil until golden.
Add garlic + ginger + mushrooms; cook 3–4 min.
Add kale, tamari, chilli, lime juice/zest; toss until wilted.
Serve over hot cauliflower rice. Finish with spring onion and extra chilli if desired.
Smoky Tofu + Black Bean Bowl with Cabbage Slaw
370 kcal · 42g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 300 g Firm tofu (calcium‑set if possible) — pressed, cubed
  • 100 g Black beans (drained)
  • 150 g Cabbage — shredded
  • 0.5 each Lime — juice
  • 1 clove Garlic — minced
  • 1 tsp Smoked paprika
  • 0.5 tsp Cumin
  • 1 tsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • 25 g Avocado — optional (small)
  • 10 g Coriander — optional
  • 1 pinch Salt + pepper — to taste
Method / flavour notes
Sear tofu with paprika + cumin + salt/pepper (1 tsp olive oil) until browned.
Warm black beans with a splash of water + garlic.
Toss cabbage with lime juice, salt, pepper (and coriander if using).
Build bowl: slaw + beans + tofu. Add a small avocado slice for creaminess (optional).
Lemon‑Garlic Tofu, Broccoli & Quinoa
350 kcal · 40g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 280 g Firm tofu (calcium‑set if possible) — pressed, cubed
  • 250 g Broccoli
  • 30 g Quinoa (dry) — (makes ~80 g cooked)
  • 0.5 each Lemon — juice + zest
  • 2 cloves Garlic — minced
  • 1 tsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • 0.5 tsp Chilli flakes — optional
  • 1 pinch Salt + pepper — to taste
Method / flavour notes
Roast or air‑fry tofu + broccoli with garlic, 1 tsp olive oil, salt/pepper.
Stir through lemon juice/zest and chilli.
Serve with cooked quinoa. Add extra lemon at the end for brightness.
Turmeric Lentil‑Spinach Dahl with Firm Tofu
360 kcal · 40g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 50 g Red lentils (dry) — rinsed
  • 220 g Firm tofu (calcium‑set if possible) — cubed
  • 120 g Spinach
  • 2 cloves Garlic — minced
  • 10 g Ginger — grated
  • 1 tsp Turmeric
  • 1 tsp Curry powder / garam masala
  • 150 ml Tomato passata
  • 250 ml Vegetable stock / water
  • 0.5 each Lemon — finish with juice
  • 1 pinch Salt — to taste
Method / flavour notes
Simmer lentils in passata + stock with turmeric, curry spice, garlic, ginger (12–15 min).
Fold in tofu cubes and spinach; cook 2–3 min to warm through.
Finish with lemon juice and salt to taste. Optional: chilli flakes.
Chickpea‑Tempeh ‘Tikka’ with Cauliflower + Spinach
370 kcal · 43g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 160 g Tempeh — crumbled or cubed
  • 90 g Chickpeas (drained)
  • 250 g Cauliflower — florets or rice
  • 120 g Spinach
  • 2 cloves Garlic — minced
  • 1.5 tsp Tikka / curry spice blend
  • 150 ml Tomato passata
  • 120 g Greek yogurt 0% (or Skyr) — stirred in at end
  • 1 pinch Salt + pepper — to taste
Method / flavour notes
Pan‑sear tempeh with tikka spice and garlic (use a splash of water instead of extra oil if needed).
Add passata and chickpeas; simmer 5–7 min.
Stir in spinach until wilted; remove from heat and fold in yogurt for creaminess.
Serve with cauliflower (rice or florets).
Beetroot‑Citrus Cottage Cheese Salad with Lentils + Walnuts
320 kcal · 39g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 300 g Low‑fat cottage cheese
  • 100 g Lentils (cooked/drained)
  • 120 g Beetroot — cooked, diced
  • 0.5 each Orange (or other citrus) — segments
  • 150 g Asparagus — blanched/roasted
  • 10 g Walnuts — crushed
  • 1 tsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp Balsamic vinegar
  • 60 g Baby spinach / greens — optional
  • 1 pinch Salt + pepper — to taste
Method / flavour notes
Toss lentils, beetroot, asparagus and citrus segments with balsamic + 1 tsp olive oil.
Top with cottage cheese and walnuts.
Season well (salt/pepper). Add greens if desired for volume.
Miso‑Ginger Edamame + Tofu Soup (Shiitake & Spinach)
310 kcal · 38g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 250 g Firm tofu (calcium‑set if possible) — cubed
  • 120 g Edamame (shelled)
  • 100 g Shiitake mushrooms — sliced
  • 120 g Spinach
  • 1 clove Garlic — minced
  • 10 g Ginger — grated
  • 1.5 tbsp Miso paste
  • 1 tsp Soy sauce / tamari — optional
  • 450 ml Vegetable stock / water
  • 1 each Spring onion — to serve
  • 0.25 each Lime — optional squeeze
Method / flavour notes
Simmer stock with garlic, ginger and mushrooms 5 minutes.
Add edamame + tofu; warm through 3–4 minutes.
Turn heat off; whisk miso with a little hot broth, then stir back in (do not boil).
Add spinach to wilt. Serve with spring onion and optional lime.
Snacks
Berry‑Vanilla Chia Skyr Pudding
190 kcal · 26g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 220 g Skyr (or Greek yogurt 0%)
  • 12 g Chia seeds
  • 5 g Flax seeds (ground) — optional
  • 100 g Berries — fresh or frozen
  • 0.5 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 0.25 each Lemon — zest (optional)
Method / flavour notes
Stir chia (and optional flax) + vanilla into Skyr; refrigerate 30–60 min (or overnight).
Top with berries and lemon zest.

Top with pumpkin seeds (optional).

Citrus‑Cocoa Protein Mousse
170 kcal · 25g protein per serving
▾
Ingredients (per serving)
  • 250 g Low‑fat cottage cheese (or Skyr)
  • 7 g Cocoa powder
  • 0.25 each Orange (or lemon) — zest
  • 1 tsp Sweetener — optional
  • 0.25 tsp Cinnamon — optional
Method / flavour notes
Blend cottage cheese with cocoa, citrus zest and sweetener until smooth.
Chill 10 minutes. Optional: dust with cinnamon and add a few berries.
Banana‑Choc Protein Muffin (1 muffin)
175 kcal · 23g protein per serving
▾
Batch makes 6 servings.
Ingredients (batch)
  • 160 g Ripe banana — mashed
  • 250 g Greek yogurt 0% (or Skyr)
  • 60 g Protein powder (whey or plant) — chocolate or vanilla
  • 15 g Cocoa powder
  • 40 g Oat flour — or fine oats
  • 2 tsp Baking powder
  • 20 g Dark chocolate — chips/shaved (optional)
  • 1 each Egg — optional binder (egg is not the main ingredient)
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract — optional
  • 1 pinch Salt
Method / flavour notes
Batch makes 6 muffins. Oven 180°C (160°C fan).
Mix banana + yogurt (+ optional egg + vanilla).
Stir in protein powder, cocoa, oat flour, baking powder, salt. Fold in chocolate chips.
Divide into 6 muffin cups. Bake 12–15 min until set. Cool before eating.
Banana Chocolate Protein Brownie (1 slice)
155 kcal · 19g protein per serving
▾
Batch makes 8 servings.
Ingredients (batch)
  • 240 g Ripe banana — mashed
  • 450 g Skyr (or Greek yogurt 0%)
  • 90 g Protein powder (whey or plant)
  • 25 g Cocoa powder
  • 40 g Oat flour
  • 2 tsp Baking powder
  • 15 g Dark chocolate — chips/shaved (optional)
  • 1 each Egg — optional for fudgier texture (not the main ingredient)
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract — optional
  • 1 pinch Salt
Method / flavour notes
Batch makes 8 slices. Line a 20 cm square tin. Oven 180°C (160°C fan).
Mix banana + Skyr (+ optional egg + vanilla). Stir in protein powder, cocoa, oat flour, baking powder, salt.
Fold in chocolate chips. Spread into tin.
Bake 18–25 min until set but still soft in the centre. Cool fully before slicing.
Optional supplements (per your notes): methylcobalamin (B12), zinc picolinate, magnesium glycinate.
